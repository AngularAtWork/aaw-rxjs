Observable
A method of returning zero to an unlimited number of values both synchronously and asynchronously.
Observer
An object that contains callbacks for each type of Observable notification: next, error and complete.
Subscribe
The act of starting an Observable’s execution and receiving its notifications.
Next
Method called to notify subscribers of a new value. Also known as ‘emitting’.
Complete
Method called to notify subscribers that the Observable will no longer pass values.
Error
Method called to notify subscribers that a problem has occurred with producing values and that the Observable will no longer pass values.
Observable Contract
During an Observable’s execution, zero to an infinite number of values can be emitted. When an Observable completes or errors, no further emissions, completions or errors can take place.
Unsubscribe
Terminates the subscriber’s receiving notifications.
Finalization
The return function of an Observable, guaranteed to be called when it completes or errors or when a subscriber unsubscribes.
Producer
Anything that is the source of the values that are emitted by the Observable.
Cold
The producer of the Observable's values is inside the Observable execution.
Hot
The producer of the Observable's values is outside the Observable execution.
Unicast
One producer is observed by one subscriber.

Cold Observables are always unicast.
Multicast
One producer is observed by many subscribers.

Hot Observables are almost always multicast.
